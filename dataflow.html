Overview

About the project

DataFlow is a business intelligence platform used by 500+ companies. Their dashboard was built by engineers without UX input — resulting in a powerful but unusable product with a 34% monthly churn rate among new users.


My mandate was to redesign the entire information architecture and reporting experience to make it accessible to non-technical users, while retaining all the power that existing analyst users relied on.

The Problem

What was broken

New users churned within 30 days at a 34% rate. Session recordings showed users spending 8+ minutes just trying to create their first report.


The navigation had 47 top-level items. Filters were hidden behind a secondary panel. The product was powerful — but completely inaccessible to non-analysts.

  • 47 top-level navigation items — no hierarchy
  • 8+ minutes average time to first report
  • Filters hidden in secondary panel
  • All chart types look identical — hard to choose
  • No onboarding or contextual guidance
Research

How I found the truth

I combined behavioural data with qualitative research to understand both what users were doing and why.

🎯
Jobs-to-be-Done Interviews
18 customers · 60 mins each · Focused on the core "jobs" users were trying to accomplish with DataFlow.
📹
Session Recording Analysis
200+ FullStory sessions reviewed. Identified the top 10 "rage click" and drop-off moments in the app.
🃏
Card Sorting
24 participants · Open card sort for navigation IA. Revealed clear mental model clusters that matched only 40% of the current structure.
🖱️
First-Click Testing
Maze · 156 participants · Tested 5 critical task flows. First-click accuracy on the existing nav was 23%.

Outcomes & Impact

The results

The redesigned IA reduced top-level nav from 47 to 8 items. The Quick Reports wizard made the 5 most common tasks accessible in under 90 seconds. Chart selection was replaced with a goal-based chooser.

After 2 months post-launch, DataFlow saw their best-ever retention numbers and a surge in new user activation.

  • Time-to-first-report dropped from 8 minutes to 90 seconds
  • 30-day churn reduced from 34% to 9%
  • First-click task accuracy rose from 23% to 89%
  • App Store rating improved from 2.9 to 4.6 stars
  • NPS rose from 18 to 52 post-launch

Reflections

What I learned

🏗️
IA is invisible — until it breaks
Users never think about navigation. They just expect to find what they need. Card sorting revealed that our mental model was completely misaligned with how users think about data tasks.
🧠
Power users need protecting
Simplifying for new users risked alienating power users. The solution: progressive disclosure — simple defaults that expand on demand. Don't dumb down, layer up.
📉
Churn is a design problem
34% monthly churn wasn't a sales or marketing issue. It was a UX problem. This project proved that good design directly impacts retention and revenue metrics.

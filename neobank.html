Overview

About the project

NeoBank is a challenger bank targeting millennials in Southeast Asia. I led end-to-end product design — from initial research through to final developer handoff — for a complete redesign of their core transaction flow, onboarding experience, and home screen.


The project ran for 6 months and involved close collaboration with a product manager, two engineers, and an in-house user researcher. I was responsible for all design decisions, from IA and flows through to final UI and design system components.

The Problem

What was broken

Users were abandoning transactions midway. The checkout flow had 7 steps, inconsistent UI patterns, and unclear error states. Support tickets cited "confusion" as the #1 reason for drop-off.


Task completion rate sat at just 54%. NPS was 32. The business was estimated to be losing $1.2M/year from failed and abandoned transactions.

  • 7-step checkout flow with high abandonment
  • No real-time transaction feedback
  • PIN re-entry required for every transaction
  • Inconsistent UI patterns across screens
  • Error messages were vague and unhelpful
Research

How I found the truth

Before touching Figma, I spent 6 weeks in deep discovery to understand what was really happening and why.

🔍
Moderated Usability Tests
12 sessions · 45 mins each · Think-aloud protocol on key transaction flows. Recruited from existing user base.
📊
Customer Support Analysis
Reviewed 800+ support tickets from the last 6 months. Coded by theme to identify the most frequent pain points.
🔬
Heuristic Evaluation
Evaluated the existing app against Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics. Identified 23 distinct violations.
🌏
Competitive Benchmarking
Audited 6 challenger banks — Revolut, Monzo, GCash, OVO, Wise, and Grab Financial. Identified best-in-class patterns.
Key Insights

What users told us

Three themes emerged clearly from research that directly shaped the design direction.

❝

"I get confused when I hit the payment screen — there are too many options and I don't know which one to choose."

❝

"I never know if my transfer actually went through. I have to check my balance again to be sure."

❝

"Why do I need to enter my PIN again for every single payment? It feels like the app doesn't trust me."

Outcomes & Impact

The results

The redesigned flow reduced checkout from 7 steps to 3. Real-time feedback states were added throughout every transaction. A biometric shortcut replaced PIN re-entry for transactions under $50.

After 3 months post-launch, the results exceeded targets across every metric. The product team considered it the most impactful redesign in the company's history.

  • Task completion rate rose from 54% to 91%
  • NPS jumped from 32 to 71 (industry benchmark: 45)
  • Support tickets dropped 62% in the first month
  • App Store rating improved from 3.1 to 4.6 stars
  • Estimated $1.2M in annual revenue recovered

Reflections

What I learned

🧩
Test earlier, fail cheaper
The biggest insight was that lo-fi testing in week 3 caught flow issues that would have been extremely costly to fix in high-fidelity. Prototype early and often.
📢
Stakeholders need showing, not telling
Bringing stakeholders into usability testing sessions directly was far more effective than sharing research reports. They saw the pain firsthand and became advocates for user needs.
📐
Design systems pay compound interest
Building a token-based design system from day 1 added overhead upfront but saved weeks of iteration time as the project scaled across multiple screens and edge cases.
